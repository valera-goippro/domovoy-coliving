Coliving · A smart home near Porto, Portugal

The house runs itself. What I'd like is good company.

I'm an older entrepreneur, and some years ago I turned this house near Porto into a proper smart home — it runs the climate, the lighting, the security and the energy by itself. My children grew up here and then scattered across different countries, the way children do, and the house became far larger than one person needs. I'm also half-disabled; I don't move around the way I used to. So rather than rent rooms to strangers, I'd like to share the house the way a home is meant to be shared — with people who will live in it well. There is no rent. What I ask for instead is a hand with the small things, and good company at the table.

01 — The idea

A household, not a rental

This is not a business and not a hostel — it is my home, and I'm inviting a few people to share it. The idea is an old one. Long before anyone called it "coliving", a big house simply held a household: several people under one roof, each doing what they could, the meals and the evenings and the work all shared. That is all this is. The smart system takes care of the building itself. The rest — the company, the small kindnesses, the sense that someone is home — is what we give each other instead of paying rent.

02 — The house

A home that quietly takes care of itself

I built Domovoy slowly, the way you build something you mean to live in for good. It is a real smart home: climate, lighting, security and energy all managed on their own, with access given and revoked through an app. It also keeps a quiet watch over the people inside it — it was built so that if someone falls, or something is wrong, it is noticed straight away. The house is large and easy to live in: a long, bright corridor, two staircases, a kitchen made for cooking together, a garden, a terrace, even a small gym. There is room here to be among people, and room to be alone — whichever the day asks for.

03 — How it works

No rent. A fair, human trade instead.

Here is the honest version, with nothing hidden. You give a little time and your presence; in return, you have a home. It is not a job — no shifts, no hours to clock. It works the way help has always worked in a family: whoever is near lends a hand.

What I ask

A little through the week, arranged easily around your own work.

Be around, and give me a hand with small things — passing me a crutch, carrying something up the stairs, the bits I can no longer rush.
A run to the shops a couple of times a week.
The odd drive when one is needed — the car is there for it.
Speak English with me. I'm still learning it, and a housemate to practise with day to day is worth a great deal.
And mostly — simply be an easy, kind, reliable person to share a home with.

No timesheet, no rota. The smart house does everything mechanical. The one thing it can't do is be human — and that is the part I'm asking you for.

What you get

A genuine base while you get on with your own work and life.

A private room that is yours — no rent, not now and not later.
Every bill covered: power, water, heating and fast internet.
Real space to work, indoors and out.
A car you are free to use.
The ocean fifteen minutes away, and the great surf coast of Nazaré within easy reach.
A warm household — and someone who knows this country well and is glad to show it to you.

A bottle of something nice is always welcome — someone turned up with kefir the other day. But gifts are never the point. The company is.

04 — Who it's for

For people who are easy to live with

  • Remote workers and freelancers who would rather not hand their salary to a Porto landlord.
  • Makers, developers, designers — anyone building something of their own.
  • Couples and families with children; the rooms are made for two.
  • Surfers and people drawn to the ocean — the coast and Nazaré are close.
  • Anyone between chapters who needs a warm, steady place to be for a while.

Probably not for you if…

…you're after a hotel, or a rental you never have to think about. This is a shared home. It works only if you're the kind of person others can count on for a small hand and honest company. If that's you, you'll feel at home here quickly.

05 — Where

Near Porto — and fifteen minutes from the ocean

The house sits a short drive outside Porto — close enough for the city's cafés, markets and coworking, far enough for quiet. The Atlantic is fifteen minutes away. This whole coast is one of the world's great surf regions; at Nazaré, just up the shoreline, the winter swell raises the biggest rideable waves on the planet.
